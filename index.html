Youth Voices for Peace

Youth Voices Standing Against Extremism

Welcome to Peace Voices, a place where youth speak up, share their experiences, and inspire peace across West Africa in the face of extremism. This project grows out of research on religious extremism and the impact of youth exchange programs like YES.

About

Peace Voices is a project for GSTR 410:
Faith and Fanatics Religious Extremism at Home and Abroad
It explores how youth, education, and exchange programs can reduce extremist recruitment in West Africa and promote peaceful coexistence.

Inspired by the YES (Youth Exchange and Study) Program‚Äîcreated after 9/11 to build bridges between cultures‚Äîthis project reflects my experience as a YES 2017 alumnus from Senegal. Living in another culture taught me how understanding and dialogue can break stereotypes and inspire peace.

Peace Voices turns this idea into action by giving young people a space to share their voices, tell their stories, and stand together against extremism.

Our Mission

Our mission is simple yet powerful: to create a safe online space where young people can learn about the roots of extremism, share their own stories of peace, and explore solutions rooted in dialogue, education, and respect.

By amplifying youth perspectives, Peace Voices reminds the world that peace begins with understanding ‚Äî and that every voice matters.

Focus Areas

  • Understanding Extremism

    Explaining why some youth are drawn into extremist groups: social pressure, unemployment, identity struggles, and manipulation of religion.

  • Youth Exchange & YES

    Highlighting how programs like YES, created after 9/11, use exchange, host families, and education to replace fear with understanding.

  • Media Literacy

    Helping youth recognize propaganda, fake religious quotes, and online hate so they can resist extremist narratives on social media.

  • Faith & Dialogue

    Encouraging honest conversations about religion, doubt, and questions in safe spaces rather than in secret extremist chats.

  • Youth Stories

    Sharing testimonies from young people who chose education, community projects, and peace instead of violent ideologies.

  • Community Action

    Showing local peace initiatives, interfaith clubs, and youth-led projects that build resilience against radicalization.

Extremist Groups in West Africa

Boko Haram

Founded in 2009 in Nigeria, Boko Haram rejects Western education and promotes a radical view of Islam. The group operates across Nigeria, Niger, Chad, and Cameroon, recruiting youth through fear, poverty, and manipulation.

Source: U.S. National Counterterrorism Center

Islamic State West Africa Province

A splinter group from Boko Haram that pledged allegiance to ISIS in 2015. It operates around the Lake Chad Basin, known for sophisticated propaganda and governance-like control over territories.

Source: New Lines Institute

Ansar Dine

Created in 2012 by Iyad ag Ghali, Ansar Dine aimed to enforce strict Sharia law in northern Mali. It became one of the key players in the 2012 Tuareg rebellion before merging into JNIM in 2017.

Source: Wikipedia

Katiba Macina

Based in central Mali, this group recruits heavily among Fulani youth. It has merged with JNIM and promotes extremist narratives using local ethnic grievances as recruitment tools.

Source: New Lines Institute
